Robin Guide — Community Consensus Build (v3.4)

Physical · Harmony · T0 Support
[Ad]

TL;DR — Quick Answer

Robin is the absolute best Harmony support in Honkai: Star Rail as of version 3.4, especially dominant in follow-up attack (FUA) compositions. Her ultimate enters the Concerto state, advancing all allies' actions by 100% and adding bonus Physical damage to every attack for the duration. Best team: Feixiao + Robin + Topaz + Aventurine. Relic priority is 4pc Prisoner in Deep Confinement or 2pc+2pc ATK sets. Her signature light cone "Flowing Nightglow" is her best-in-slot but "Bronya's Light Cone" (But the Battle Isn't Over) is a strong alternative.

Community Consensus: How Good Is Robin?

Robin has been rated SS-tier (T0) by every major community tier list since her debut in version 2.2. In Memory of Chaos 3.4 data aggregated from Prydwen and community spreadsheet trackers, Robin appears on over 65% of all zero-cycle and two-cycle clears — the highest representation of any character in endgame content. She is considered the defining support of the FUA meta alongside Feixiao, and her power level has not diminished with subsequent patches.

Community polls on r/HonkaiStarRail consistently rank Robin as the best overall support alongside Ruan Mei, with the edge in any team built around follow-up attacks. Her ability to advance the entire party's actions simultaneously is unique — no other character offers a team-wide 100% action advance.

How Her Kit Works (Community-Verified Mechanics)

Robin's kit revolves around her ultimate ability and the unique Concerto state. Here's how it works:

  • Skill: Robin deals Physical damage to a single target and advances her own next action by 25%. She also regenerates 5 energy. The skill is primarily used for energy generation and damage contribution.
  • Ultimate (Concerto): Robin enters the Concerto state for 90 AV (Action Value). During Concerto, all allies are immediately advanced by 100% — they take an extra turn. While in Concerto, all allies deal additional bonus Physical damage equal to 50% of Robin's ATK on every attack. Robin cannot act while in Concerto; she exits when duration expires or if she is knocked down.
  • Talent: When allies attack, Robin regenerates 2 energy. This is doubled during Concerto to 4 energy per ally attack. In FUA teams, this means a single Feixiao FUA sequence can refund 8-12 energy.
  • Technique: At the start of battle, Robin immediately regenerates 50% of her ultimate energy.

Key community-verified interaction: Robin's Concerto action advance does not consume the ally's next turn — it gives them a bonus turn. This means Feixiao gets an extra FUA charge generation cycle, Topaz gets an extra Numby turn, and debuff durations all tick down on the bonus turn as expected.

Dual DPS Hypercarry

Robin can also slot into dual carry compositions where her team-wide advance enables two DPS characters to attack before enemies act. Pairs well with Clara + Yunli or Blade + Jingliu teams.

DOT Team Variant

While less common, Robin can work in DoT teams with Kafka + Black Swan. The 100% team-wide action advance lets DoTs tick an extra time, and her bonus Physical damage adds meaningful chip damage. However, Ruan Mei is generally preferred here for her RES penetration and extended break duration.

Relic & Light Cone Priority (Tested Rankings)

Relic Sets

  1. 4pc Prisoner in Deep Confinement — Best for Robin. 12% ATK from 2pc, plus the 4pc effect ignores up to 18% DEF. However, Robin deals very little personal damage, so the ATK is the main draw.
  2. 2pc Messenger + 2pc ATK% — Strong alternative. 6% SPD helps Robin take turns naturally, and 12% ATK boosts the bonus damage from Concerto.
  3. 2pc ATK% + 2pc ATK% (Passerby/Hackerspace/Musketeer) — Budget option. Maximizes ATK for Concerto bonus damage.

Planar Ornaments

  1. Fleet of the Ageless — Best support choice. 12% ATK + 8% party-wide ATK when Robin reaches 120 SPD.
  2. Broken Keel — Alternative. 10% CRIT DMG + 10% party-wide CRIT DMG if Robin has 30%+ Effect RES.
  3. Vonwacq's Squirrel — Viable for faster first rotation. 5% energy regeneration + 40% action advance at battle start.

Main Stats

  • Body: ATK% (primary stat)
  • Feet: ATK% or SPD (SPD to hit 120 for Fleet of Ageless)
  • Rope: Energy Regeneration Rate (ERR) — absolutely required for smooth ult rotation
  • Sphere: ATK%

Frequently Asked Questions

Robin E1 vs S1 — which should I pull?

S1 first, then E1. Her signature light cone "Flowing Nightglow" provides significant energy regeneration and team-wide damage buffs that smooth out her rotation. E1 gives 24% RES penetration to the entire team, which is an excellent damage increase, but the S1's energy and utility make it the higher-prio pull. Community consensus: S1 > E1 > E2.

What ATK breakpoint should Robin reach?

Aim for 4000+ ATK with buffs active. Since Robin's Concerto bonus damage scales with her ATK (50% of ATK per hit), maximizing ATK is her primary build goal. In combat with Fleet of Ageless and her trace ATK bonuses, this is achievable with ATK% body, ATK% sphere, and 2-3 ATK% substat rolls per piece. Outgoing ATK (before combat) should be around 3000-3500.

Does Robin need speed?

Only if using Fleet of Ageless (needs 120 SPD). Robin spends most of the battle in Concerto, during which she cannot act, making speed boots less valuable than ATK% boots. However, if you run Fleet of Ageless, you need exactly 120 SPD (base 102 + 9 from traces = 111; you need 9 SPD from substats or 2pc Messenger). Many players prefer ATK% boots and running Vonwacq or Broken Keel instead.

Can Robin work outside FUA teams?

Yes, but she's not the best option. Robin's Concerto is strong in any team composition due to the 100% action advance. However, her energy regeneration talent relies on allies attacking — FUA teams double this by generating more attack instances per turn. In non-FUA teams (hypercarry, DoT), her ultimate uptime drops. She's still usable but Ruan Mei or Sparkle may perform better depending on the team.
