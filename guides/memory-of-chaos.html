📋 What Is Memory of Chaos?

Memory of Chaos (MoC) is Honkai: Star Rail's flagship endgame mode — the ultimate test of account strength, team-building skill, and in-depth understanding of combat mechanics. It resets twice per month, on the 1st and the 16th, with a new turbulence effect (the "Memory Turbulence") that reshapes the meta for that cycle.

The mode consists of 12 sequential floors, each split into two halves. You field two separate teams (one per half), and both halves share a total cycle count of 20. To earn the full 3★ on a floor, you must complete both halves within 20 cumulative cycles. The run ends immediately once either team wipes — there is no retry within the same run.

The difficulty ramp is steep. Floors 1-9 are straightforward for most accounts with level 80 characters and maxed traces. Floors 10-12 demand specific team archetypes, correct weakness coverage, precise speed tuning, and deep roster depth. Enemy HP pools on MoC 12 exceed 2 million HP per wave in v3.4, making every optimization critical.

⚡ Current v3.4 Turbulence — "Crystalline Storm"

Every MoC cycle features a Memory Turbulence — a global buff that applies to both halves of every floor. The v3.4 turbulence is named Crystalline Storm, and it heavily favors Follow-Up Attack (FUA) and Break compositions.

🌀 Crystalline Storm — Full Mechanics

  • Trigger: An ally lands a Follow-Up Attack (FUA) against a Weakness-Broken enemy.
  • Effect: Deals 50% True Damage (ignores DEF and RES) to the target, split across all enemies in an AoE. True Damage cannot be mitigated by enemy mechanics.
  • Bonus: The attacking ally regenerates 15 Energy immediately after the proc.
  • Additional MoC 12 bonus: Ally weakness break or debuff application increases FUA and Break DMG by 30% for 2 turns.

💡 Crystalline Storm Strategy: The turbulence rewards a specific play pattern: Break enemies fast → spam FUA while broken. Each FUA proc not only deals massive True Damage but also recharges the attacker's Energy by 15, creating a positive feedback loop. A single rotation from Feixiao + Topaz + Aventurine can generate 300K–500K bonus True Damage through turbulence procs alone on MoC 12. Pair a fast breaker (Ruan Mei, HMC, Fugue) with a FUA DPS to maximize the window.

A fully built FUA team clears MoC 12 in 2-4 cycles with consistent reliability. The synergy with the turbulence is so strong that even budget FUA teams outperform many premium Hypercarry teams.

⏱️ Cycle Management & Speed Tuning

In MoC, each "cycle" is essentially 100 Action Value (AV). The total AV budget for both halves combined is 2,000 AV (20 cycles × 100 AV). Every optimization counts — shaving 1 cycle from each half can be the difference between 2★ and 3★.

Key Breakpoints Explained:

  • 134 SPD: The minimum recommended breakpoint for any unit. Gives you 2 actions in the first 2 cycles (100 AV → only 1 turn at cycle 1, but 2nd turn arrives before cycle 2 ends).
  • 143 SPD: Gives your 1st action earlier in cycle 0, allowing DPS to move before enemy turn 1 and potentially kill the first wave before they act (0-cycle wave clear).
  • 160+ SPD: The "2-turn opening" threshold. Your unit takes 2 actions before cycle 1 ends, dramatically accelerating break windows, energy generation, and turbulence procs.
  • 164+ SPD: The "3-turn in 2 cycles" breakpoint. Essential for Firefly (who self-buffs SPD in her Enhanced state) and Kafka (who needs to reapply DoT frequently).

Pro Cycle Management Tips

  1. Pre-charge All Ultimate before MoC 12: Enter the floor with full energy on every character. Use lower floors (MoC 9 or earlier) to pre-charge. A full team ult rotation in wave 1 can kill the first wave in 0 cycles, saving 2+ cycles.
  2. Weakness Match Religiously: If your team matches fewer than 2/3 of the enemy's weaknesses (after optimization), you are losing 1-2 full cycles to Toughness damage penalties. Swap teams, not characters. It's better to field an E0 A-tier team with perfect weakness coverage than a mismatched S-tier team.
  3. Skip Ultimate Animations: Press ESC or the skip button during Ultimate animations. Each Ult skipped saves ~2 seconds. Over the course of 20+ Ultimates in a MoC 12 run, that's 40+ seconds — enough for 1-2 extra turns late in the fight that could secure a full ★.
  4. Know When to Reset: If your first half completes in 5+ cycles at MoC 12, do not continue the run — reset immediately. Each half should take ≤4 cycles for full 3★. A 5-cycle first half + 4-cycle second half = 19 cycles total, which barely passes but leaves no margin for error. The energy and frustration of clawing back from a bad start is rarely worth it.
  5. Plan Your Wave 0 Timing: Entering wave 2 with full team action value (i.e., all turns queued up immediately) is the difference between a clean clear and a slow slog. When finishing wave 1, try to end on a support's turn (Tingyun, Bronya) rather than your DPS — this lets the DPS enter wave 2 with maximum effective AV.
  6. AoE-first on Multi-Add Waves: True Sting, Yanqing, and Aurumaton all spawn adds. If your team lacks AoE, the adds will multiply faster than you can kill them, resulting in a wipe. Bring at least one blast or AoE character to every floor with multiple enemies. Herta, Argenti, Firefly, and Feixiao all qualify.
  7. Wave Management — The Speed Trick: In wave 1, always kill the fastest enemy last. When the wave transitions, leftover Action Values carry over. If you kill the last enemy on one character's turn, that character gets their full AV for the next wave immediately. Use this to frontload damage on the boss.

🔄 Advanced Rotation & Energy Management

Energy management is the single biggest differentiator between a 3-cycle and a 6-cycle clear. Every ultimate wasted on a support without DPS follow-up is a cycle lost.

Energy Generation Sources

  • Energy Recharge Rope (ERR): Every support must run an ERR rope. The 19.4% ERR bonus transforms 3-turn Ultimates into 2-turn Ultimates for most Harmony characters. Robin specifically needs ~120% ERR for a guaranteed 3-turn rotation (Ult → Skill → Skill).
  • Huohuo's Ultimate: Restores 20% Energy to all allies. On a team of 4, this is effectively an 80% Energy refund across the team. Huohuo is mandatory for any team that struggles with energy economy (Acheron without Jiaoqiu, Jingliu, DoT).
  • Planar Set ERR: Vonwacq (5% ERR + turn 1 action advance), Penacony/Lushaka (5% ERR + DMG% for matching type). These planar sets are the difference between a 3-turn and 4-turn Ult cycle.
  • Crystalline Storm Energy: In v3.4, each FUA proc on a broken enemy grants +15 Energy. For Aventurine (who triggers FUA every time an ally is attacked or uses Follow-up), this means he can Ult every 2 turns instead of 4. Factor this into your rotation planning.
  • Light Cone Energy: Carve the Moon (Battle Pass) provides random Energy on some rotations. Quid Pro Quo (gacha 4★) gives Energy to the lowest-Energy ally. These can shift critical Ult timing — use them wisely.

Ultimate Timing Rules

  1. Never press a support's Ultimate unless your DPS's turn is next (or queued immediately after). Wasting buff uptime on a healer or sustainer's turn is the #1 cycle-wasting mistake. Wait for the AV order to line up.
  2. Chain Ultimates from fastest to slowest. Example: Robin (fast) → Feixiao (slow). The buff from the first Ult applies to the second Ult, and the fast unit gets their next turn sooner.
  3. Don't overwrite buffs. Robin's Ult lasts 2 turns. If you chain Robin Ult → Feixiao Ult → Robin Ult again (because you farmed too much energy with ERR), you overwrite the Concerto state. Time your second Robin Ult to start exactly when the first ends.
  4. Save Huohuo's Ult for DPS Ult windows. Using Huohuo Ult → Feixiao immediately Ults → Feixiao gets +20% energy → Feixiao's next Ult comes 1 turn sooner. This chain is worth 2 cycles over a full run.

🍃 Example: Feixiao FUA Rotation Sequence

Turn 1: Robin Skill → Feixiao Skill (triggers Topaz FUA).
Turn 2: Aventurine FUA (from enemy attack) → Crystalline Storm proc (+15 Energy to Aventurine).
Turn 3: Robin Ult (with ERR rope) → Feixiao Skill (again) → Feixiao Ult → Numby FUA → Crystalline Storm procs on all hits.
Turn 4: Feixiao's Ult gauge is already 60%+ from Crystalline Storm energy. One more Skill and she's ready to Ult again.

This rotation shaves 2 full cycles compared to using Feixiao's Ult without managing Robin's Concerto uptime. In MoC 12, this sequence alone deals ~1.5M damage plus turbulence True Damage.

❓ Memory of Chaos FAQ

Q: When does Memory of Chaos reset?

MoC resets twice per month: on the 1st and the 16th at server reset time (04:00 server time). You have approximately 15 days per cycle to achieve full stars.

Q: Do I need to re-clear all 12 floors each reset?

No. Each reset, you keep your 3★ completions from the previous cycle. You only need to re-clear any floors where you scored less than 3★ (or skipped entirely). However, the turbulence, enemy lineups, and boss rotations change every reset, so prior strategies may not apply.

Q: Can I retry a half without resetting the other half?

No. MoC runs both halves sequentially. If team 1 fails, the entire run resets. However, you can exit the run and start over at any time — only your best score per floor is saved.

Q: Are the 20 cycles shared or per-half?

Shared. Both halves combined must complete within 20 cycles for 3★. If your first half takes 6 cycles, your second half has only 14 cycles remaining. This makes it critical to balance clear speeds between both teams.

Q: Do supports' Light Cone effects (like Quid Pro Quo's Energy transfer) work across both halves?

No. Each half is a completely separate battle with no cross-over. Teams are independent in all respects. You cannot "battery" team 1's energy into team 2.

Q: Is there a difference between MoC 12 and the older Swarm Disaster / Gold and Gears?

Yes — those are Simulated Universe (SU) expansions, not MoC. MoC 12 is the current pinnacle of Memory of Chaos difficulty. The Swarm and Gold & Gears modes are permanent SU content with different scaling, blessings, and rewards.

Q: How important is signature Light Cone for S-tier characters?

For most S-tier characters, the difference between F2P and S1 is 10-15% damage at most. The only exception is Acheron — her S1 (Along the Passing Shore) provides a massive 24% CRIT DMG and 60% Ultimate DMG boost, making it a much larger upgrade than typical. Robin, Ruan Mei, Feixiao, and Firefly are all perfectly functional at E0S0 with Herta Shop or gacha 4★ Light Cones.

Q: I'm missing [X character]. Can I still full-clear MoC 12?

In v3.4, if you are missing either Robin or Ruan Mei, full 3★ clears are very difficult but not impossible. You can substitute Bronya for Robin (slower, but functional with speed tuning), or Asta E6 for Ruan Mei (less break efficiency, but SPD buff helps cycle management). Missing both is likely to cost 1-2★ on MoC 12. Consider pulling one of them on their next rerun banner — both are expected to rerun frequently.

Q: How many cycles should I aim for per half on MoC 12?

For full 3★ (20 total cycles across both halves):
First Half: 2-4 cycles (AoE wave clear, Crystalline Storm fuel).
Second Half: 3-5 cycles (boss duel, requires sustained damage).
Total: 5-9 cycles — well within the 20-cycle budget. The remaining ~11 cycles are your safety margin for RNG (missed crits, bad AV order).
